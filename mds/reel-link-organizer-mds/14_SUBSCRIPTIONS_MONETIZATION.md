# Subscription and Monetization

## Current recommendation
Do not monetize the first prototype. First prove repeated saving and retrieval behavior.

## Free tier
- Up to a reasonable item limit
- Core search
- Basic collections
- Manual and share-sheet saving
- Standard metadata extraction
- Private collections
- Limited shared collection links

## Premium tier ideas
- Unlimited saves
- Advanced search
- OCR from screenshots
- Automatic duplicate merging
- Reel deletion alerts
- More shared collections
- Smart resurfacing
- Bulk import
- Export formats
- Custom categories and tags
- Priority metadata refresh
- Semantic search

## Mobile payments
Use RevenueCat to unify:
- Apple App Store subscriptions
- Google Play subscriptions
- Entitlement state
- Restore purchases
- Trial management

## Web payments
Use Stripe only after deciding whether web subscriptions are necessary. Respect app store rules around directing users to external purchase options.

## Pricing principle
Do not charge for the basic ability to recover a saved link. Charge for scale, automation, monitoring, and advanced retrieval.

## Monetization risk
If the free tier is too limited, users will never build enough habit to understand the value. If the free tier is unlimited forever, subscription conversion may be weak. The likely balance is generous free usage with premium automation.
